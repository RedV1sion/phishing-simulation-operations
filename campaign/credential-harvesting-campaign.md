# Objective

The objective of this phishing simulation campaign was to assess employee susceptibility to credential harvesting attacks targeting Microsoft 365 accounts and improve organizational phishing awareness.

This campaign simulated a realistic password expiration notification commonly used by threat actors to harvest enterprise credentials.

---

# Simulated Attack Scenario

Employees received a phishing email impersonating the IT department requesting immediate password verification due to an upcoming password expiration.

The email directed users to a simulated Microsoft 365 login landing page designed to test:

- User awareness
- Link inspection behavior
- Credential submission susceptibility
- Phishing reporting behavior

---

```text
[Action Required] Your Microsoft 365 Password Will Expire Today
```

---

# Key Findings

## Positive Findings

- Majority of users avoided credential submission
- User reporting rates improved compared to previous campaigns
- MFA awareness reduced successful credential entry attempts

---

## Security Concerns Identified

- Several users clicked phishing links without verifying sender legitimacy
- Finance users demonstrated higher phishing susceptibility
- Some remote users accessed phishing emails from unmanaged personal devices
- Users showed increased susceptibility to urgency-based messaging

---

# Remediation Actions

Users who failed the simulation were automatically enrolled in additional security awareness training covering:

- Credential harvesting attacks
- Microsoft 365 phishing threats
- URL inspection techniques
- MFA security practices
- Reporting suspicious emails

Additional targeted phishing simulations were scheduled for repeat offenders.

---

# Realistic Operational Challenges

The campaign identified several operational challenges commonly seen in enterprise healthcare environments:

- Hybrid workforce using personal devices
- Shared workstations in clinical areas
- High email volume causing alert fatigue
- Users responding quickly to urgent IT-themed messages
- Limited phishing reporting consistency among departments

---

# Lessons Learned

The campaign demonstrated that credential harvesting remains one of the highest-risk attack vectors in healthcare environments.

Results highlighted the importance of:

- Continuous phishing simulations
- User-focused remediation training
- MFA adoption
- Department-specific awareness campaigns
- Ongoing phishing metric analysis
