# Objective

The objective of this phishing simulation campaign was to evaluate employee susceptibility to financial fraud and Business Email Compromise (BEC)-style phishing attacks targeting invoice processing and payment workflows.

The campaign simulated a realistic urgent vendor payment request commonly used by threat actors to compromise financial operations.

---

# Simulated Attack Scenario

Employees received an email impersonating a trusted vendor requesting immediate payment review for an overdue invoice.

The phishing email included:

- Fake invoice attachment
- Urgent payment request
- Executive escalation language
- Simulated payment portal link

The simulation tested employee ability to:

- Detect suspicious financial requests
- Verify sender legitimacy
- Validate payment procedures
- Report suspicious vendor communications

# Key Findings

## Positive Findings

- Users demonstrated improved suspicious email reporting behavior
- MFA protections reduced credential harvesting attempts
- Several users escalated suspicious invoices for verification

---

## Security Concerns Identified

- Urgent financial requests generated high engagement rates
- Users trusted vendor branding without verification
- Some employees attempted invoice review without validating sender legitimacy
- Executive escalation language increased user response urgency

---

# Remediation Actions

Users who failed the simulation were assigned additional security awareness training covering:

- Business Email Compromise (BEC)
- Vendor impersonation attacks
- Financial fraud phishing techniques
- Invoice verification procedures
- Secure payment approval processes
- Suspicious email reporting procedures

Repeat offenders were added to additional high-risk phishing simulations.

---

# Security Improvements Recommended

| Recommendation | Priority |
|---|---|
| Increase BEC awareness training | High |
| Improve vendor verification procedures | High |
| Require secondary approval for urgent payments | High |
| Expand finance-specific phishing simulations | Medium |
| Reinforce payment validation workflows | Medium |

---

# Realistic Operational Challenges

The campaign identified operational challenges commonly seen in enterprise financial operations:

- Employees responding quickly to urgent payment requests
- Heavy reliance on email-based invoice processing
- High trust in vendor communications
- Pressure to avoid payment delays
- Increased phishing susceptibility during busy financial periods

---

# Lessons Learned

The campaign demonstrated that financial scam phishing attacks remain highly effective due to urgency, authority pressure, and operational workflows involving invoice processing.

Key lessons included:

- BEC attacks remain a major enterprise threat
- Finance-focused phishing simulations are highly valuable
- Vendor verification procedures must be reinforced
- Executive impersonation increases phishing engagement rates
