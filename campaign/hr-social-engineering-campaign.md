# Objective

The objective of this phishing simulation campaign was to assess employee susceptibility to Human Resources-themed social engineering attacks and improve awareness of phishing attempts impersonating internal departments.

The campaign simulated a realistic HR communication involving updated PTO and hybrid work policy changes requiring immediate employee review.

---

# Simulated Attack Scenario

Employees received a phishing email appearing to originate from the Human Resources department requesting acknowledgement of updated PTO and remote work policy changes.

The email included:

- HR impersonation
- Urgent policy acknowledgment request
- Embedded phishing link
- Simulated credential harvesting landing page

The campaign tested employee ability to:

- Verify sender legitimacy
- Detect suspicious URLs
- Recognize urgency-based social engineering
- Report suspicious communications

# Key Findings

## Positive Findings

- Improved phishing reporting compared to previous campaigns
- Users demonstrated increased caution toward unknown attachments
- MFA awareness reduced successful credential submissions

---

## Security Concerns Identified

- HR-themed phishing emails generated high engagement rates
- Remote employees demonstrated increased phishing susceptibility
- Several users trusted internal branding without validating sender legitimacy
- Users reacted quickly to urgency-based messaging involving policy compliance

---

# Remediation Actions

Users who failed the simulation were automatically enrolled in additional security awareness training focused on:

- Social engineering awareness
- HR impersonation attacks
- Credential harvesting threats
- URL verification
- Email validation techniques
- Reporting suspicious communications

Repeat offenders were added to targeted phishing simulation groups for additional monitoring.

# Realistic Operational Challenges

The campaign identified operational challenges commonly found in enterprise environments:

- Hybrid workforce dependence on email communication
- High trust in internal HR messaging
- Alert fatigue from frequent policy notifications
- Limited verification of internal sender addresses
- Increased phishing susceptibility during policy update periods

---

# Lessons Learned

The campaign demonstrated that HR-themed phishing attacks remain highly effective due to employee trust in internal communications and urgency-based messaging.

Key lessons included:

- Employees frequently trust familiar departments without verification
- Internal branding significantly increases phishing engagement
- Continuous awareness training remains essential
- Department-specific phishing scenarios improve awareness effectiveness
