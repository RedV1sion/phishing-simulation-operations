# Executive Summary

During Q2 2026, multiple phishing simulation campaigns were conducted to evaluate organizational phishing susceptibility, improve security awareness, and identify high-risk user behavior trends.

The phishing operations program focused on:

- Credential harvesting simulations
- HR social engineering attacks
- IT impersonation phishing
- Financial fraud/BEC simulations
- Data breach alert phishing

Overall results demonstrated improved phishing awareness and reporting behavior compared to previous reporting periods; however, elevated risk remained among remote users and high-risk departments.

# Highest Risk Campaign Types

## 1. Data Breach Alert Campaign

Highest engagement rates were observed during breach-themed phishing simulations involving:

- Suspicious account activity notifications
- Urgent password reset requests
- Security incident messaging
- Internal security impersonation

Users demonstrated elevated susceptibility to urgency and fear-based messaging.

---

## 2. IT Impersonation Campaigns

IT-themed phishing campaigns generated increased interaction rates due to:

- Trust in internal IT communications
- MFA-related messaging
- Microsoft branding familiarity
- Fear of account suspension

# Operational Challenges Identified

The phishing operations program identified several enterprise operational challenges:

- Hybrid workforce reliance on email communication
- Increased phishing susceptibility among remote users
- Alert fatigue from frequent security messaging
- High trust in internal IT and HR communications
- Inconsistent suspicious email reporting behavior
